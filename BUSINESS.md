# NJ Market Map — Business Model

*A working plan, not a promise. Written July 2026. Revisit it as real usage comes in.*

## What this is

**The New Jersey real-estate investor's map.** One screen that answers "where should I
look, and is the trend moving my way?" — scored by zip, town, and county, with weekly-fresh
data, for people deciding where to buy, rent out, or flip.

**Who it's for (in order):**
1. Small/individual real-estate investors (buy-and-hold, house-hackers, first flippers) who
   today squint at spreadsheets or pay for tools like PropStream/Mashvisor.
2. Agents and curious locals who want a fast read on a market.
3. Later: people outside NJ, as the map expands state by state.

**The goal:** grow it into something real *and* have it stand as a portfolio piece. Those
pull the same direction — "I built a thing people actually use" is the strongest possible
showcase, so every growth move below also makes the portfolio stronger.

## The honest picture

**Edge:** it's faster, cleaner, and more investor-focused than digging through Zillow's raw
data yourself; it's free; and it can improve quickly (an AI builds features on demand, which
most solo builders can't match).

**Risk:** the underlying data is free and public, so the *data* isn't a moat — anyone could
rebuild the map. Incumbents (Zillow, Redfin, Mashvisor, PropStream, DealMachine) are bigger
and better funded. So the real moats have to be built, not assumed:
- **Depth** — become *the* best NJ investor map, then best-in-each-state as you expand.
- **Audience** — an email list you own; competitors can't take it and you don't rent it.
- **Speed** — ship what users ask for faster than a big company can.

**The one rule that follows from this:** don't chase revenue before you have traffic. The
first job is proving people show up and come back. Money plans on top of zero users are guesses.

## The staged model

### Stage 1 — Make it spread (now)
Every share is both a new visitor *and* a portfolio impression.
- **Shareable place pages:** a real link for each town/county (e.g. `/town/hoboken`) with a
  rich preview card (name, score, headline stat, a snapshot image) so a link posted to
  Reddit / Facebook groups / X looks great and pulls clicks.
- **"Share this market" button** on the detail panel.
- **A strong About/Methodology story** — the honest, well-explained methodology *is* the
  trust pitch and the portfolio centerpiece.

*Cost: $0. This is the highest-leverage work for a grow-and-showcase product.*

### Stage 2 — Capture the audience (next)
- **Email signup** ("get the weekly NJ market update").
- **Weekly "NJ Market Pulse" newsletter** — the Thursday data pipeline already generates
  everything; the newsletter nearly writes itself (biggest movers, hottest towns, rate
  update). This is the asset a *serious* business is built on.
- Free tools that need an email (a printable market report, a watchlist) feed the list.

*Cost: near-$0 (free email tier to start, e.g. Buttondown/MailerLite).*

### Stage 3 — Monetize (once there's steady traffic)
- **Freemium subscription (~$8–15/mo):** map stays free forever; paid unlocks **alerts**
  (a town you watch drops in price or score), **watchlists**, **CSV/PDF export**, **deeper
  history**, and **more states** as they launch. Investors pay for tools that save time or
  surface deals.
- **Affiliate income alongside:** links to tools investors already buy — lenders, landlord/
  tenant-screening software, insurance. Earns even at modest traffic, no support burden.

### Stage 4 — The big prize (later, needs scale)
- **Agent & lender lead generation** — refer users to local agents/lenders/property managers
  for a fee. This is the highest-revenue model in real estate (it's Zillow's core business),
  but it needs real volume and partnerships, so it comes *after* an audience exists.

## Next 3 build steps (each also strengthens the portfolio)

1. **Shareable place pages + preview cards** — turns the map into something that spreads on
   its own, and gives you dozens of polished, linkable pages to show off.
2. **Email capture + first newsletter issue** — starts the owned audience; proves you can
   run a real product loop, not just ship a demo.
3. **A simple visits counter / analytics** (privacy-friendly, free) — so decisions about what
   to build next are driven by what people actually use, not guesses.

Only after those show real, repeat usage does the paid tier become worth building.

## What success looks like at each stage
- **Stage 1:** people share links; visits grow week over week without you posting daily.
- **Stage 2:** the email list grows steadily; newsletter open rates hold up.
- **Stage 3:** a small but real number of people pay — even 50–100 subscribers validates it.
- **Stage 4:** enough traffic that agents/lenders want to pay to reach your audience.
